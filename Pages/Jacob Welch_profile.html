About Me

I enjoy playing games just as much as I enjoy creating them. This is surprising even to me since I have been playing games daily from the age of six. My primary passion is programming and optimizing games, but I also enjoy creating videos with Adobe Premiere. My first coding experience in high school sparked my excitement for this industry. We were briefly taught to use python, and I became fascinated with getting programs to work properly. The never-ending cycle of having a problem to solve and finding the correct solution is something that I love.

Currently, I am a Senior at Bradley University where I have gotten over 4 years of experience creating games inside of the Unity game engine. Video games I have made using Unity include State Shift, Asteroids, a platformer, an infinite runner, a tower defense game, and a few other various projects. These were done in my primary major of Game Design, but I also have done coding projects for my second major, Computer Information Systems. Most of these have involved data structures such as linked stacks, linked queues, and linked trees.

Career Goals

My goal after I get my undergraduate degrees is very open at the moment. I am looking into a few main options though. The first is to get a job/internship in the gaming industry. This is likely my long-term goal for the rest of my life because games are the thing that I love in life. I enjoy playing them daily and have just as much fun making them so it only makes sense that that would be my most important goal. That being said, the gaming industry is very difficult to get into which is why I am also looking at software development jobs and internships. These are a great second alternative because I also enjoy programming a lot and think they could serve as a great stepping stone towards getting myself into the gaming industry. Having more professional experience in any development capacity will look better to hiring managers in the game industry. Lastly, my fall back option is to go for my master's degree in game design. I likely won't be getting it at Bradley University, but I can't say where yet because I am still in the early steps of researching different options.
